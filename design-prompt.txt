Here's a comprehensive prompt you can hand directly to an AI agent:

Prompt: Design System Overhaul — Eliminate AI Aesthetic Defaults
Role: You are a senior design engineer at a boutique studio. Your job is to audit and completely restyle this website's design system. The goal is to make it look like it was made by a thoughtful human designer with a specific point of view — not generated by an AI.

PHASE 1 — Research & Aesthetic Direction
Before touching any code, use web search to research 3–5 distinct design directions that feel current, specific, and non-generic. Look at references like:

Award-winning agency sites (Awwwards, Lapa Ninja, Siteinspire)
High-end editorial design (print-to-web translations)
Niche cultural verticals relevant to this product's subject matter

From your research, select one clear aesthetic direction and justify it in 2–3 sentences. Name the direction (e.g. "Swiss editorial with brutalist tension" or "Japanese spatial restraint"). This direction must be specific enough that it could not accidentally apply to a random other product.

PHASE 2 — Token Audit
Read all existing global design tokens. For each category, note what the current values signal emotionally and whether they contribute to the "AI default" look. Flag any of the following:

Color: Generic purple/blue gradients, teal accents, soft "friendly" neutrals with no backbone
Typography: Overused system stacks, Inter/Geist/Plus Jakarta for everything, no typographic hierarchy personality
Spacing: Perfectly even padding everywhere, no rhythm or tension
Border radius: Excessive rounding (>12px on cards), everything equally soft
Shadows: Layered diffuse box-shadows used as depth rather than structure
Motion: Fade-up on scroll applied universally, no motion personality


PHASE 3 — Token Replacement
Replace the flagged tokens with a new system derived from your chosen direction. Deliver:
Color — 4–6 named hex values with roles:

1 dominant field color (could be white, off-white, near-black, or a chromatic field — not default #0f0f0f or #fafafa)
1–2 accent colors (specific, not "brand blue")
1 text color and 1 secondary text color
No gradients unless the direction explicitly calls for one gradient used with restraint in a single place

Typography — Specify at least 2 typeface roles with rationale:

A display face with actual character (not Inter, not Geist, not Plus Jakarta Sans)
A body face that complements it without competing
A utility face if appropriate (monospace, condensed, etc.)
Set a real type scale: sizes, weights, line-heights, letter-spacing per role

Spacing — Define a scale with intentional rhythm, not just multiples of 4px
Radius — Assign specific values by component type. Cards should not feel like bubbles.
Shadow / Elevation — Use border or color contrast as the primary depth tool; shadows are accent-only

PHASE 4 — Module Audit & Conversion
Examine every component/module on the site. For each one, identify which of these AI design tropes it uses and replace it:

For each module: describe the problem, propose the fix, implement it.

PHASE 5 — Signature Element
Define one signature element — a single design detail that makes the site memorable and that embodies the aesthetic direction. This is your one place to spend boldness. Everything else should be quieter than this. Examples:

A typographic treatment in the hero that does something unexpected with size or weight contrast
A structural device (rule, label system, grid break) used consistently and meaningfully
A color moment that appears once and earns its place

State what it is and why it fits before implementing it.

PHASE 6 — Self-Critique
Before finalizing, run this checklist:

 Does any token feel like a default I'd choose for any random tech product?
 Is there a gradient where there doesn't need to be?
 Is Inter or a visual equivalent doing too much work?
 Are cards being used as a container when the content could simply live in space?
 Are there more than 2 scroll animations?
 Is the CTA section generic ("Ready to get started?")?
 Does the hero feel templated?
 Is the spacing rhythmically interesting or just consistently even?

Fix any "yes" answers before delivering.

Deliverables

Updated global token file
Each revised module/component
A short design rationale doc: direction chosen, research references, token decisions, signature element


Constraint: Do not reference the AI aesthetic as a failure — treat this as a positive design brief toward a specific point of view. Every choice should be justifiable, not just "because it's different."
